# Contributing

Thanks for your interest in Apogee.

Apogee is open source under GPL-3.0-or-later, so you are free to use it, fork it, study it, and build
on it. Code contribution, though, is invitation-only: pull requests are merged from a small group of
collaborators rather than taken from the open public.

## Why

A few honest reasons, none of them about the quality of your work:

- **It has a clear direction.** I have a specific idea of where Apogee is going and how it should be
  built, and I want to steer that deliberately. Even good unsolicited contributions tend to pull a
  small project in several directions at once, and I would rather keep it coherent.
- **Clean-room provenance.** Apogee is a from-scratch reimplementation that deliberately never copies
  from GPL or other existing launcher code. Keeping the contributor group small lets me stay certain of
  where every line came from, which keeps the project legally clean.
- **It handles credentials, and it is personal.** The launcher signs into your Square Enix account and
  manages secrets, and I maintain it in my spare time, so I keep the group small and people I trust.

## Want to help?

Reach out on Discord (soleynn.x) and tell me what you would like to work on. If it is a fit,
I will add you as a collaborator. I would rather bring people in on purpose than run an open queue, and
I appreciate you asking first.

## Found a bug?

Bug reports and ideas are always welcome, no invitation needed. Open an issue with the template.
